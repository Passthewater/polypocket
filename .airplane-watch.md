## Conclusion

**Numbers (id > 19, post-restart with `FOK_SLIPPAGE_TICKS=6`):**
- 18 attempts: 8 settled, 9 rejected, 1 still open.
- Cumulative reject rate: **9/17 = 53%** on closed outcomes.
- Per-iteration reject rate: iter 1 = 71% (5/7), iter 2 cumulative = 57% (8/14), iter 3 cumulative = 53% (9/17). Trend is flat — the apparent "improvement" across iterations is just dilution as the bad early-restart streak averages with later trades, not the band working harder over time.
- **PnL on 8 settled trades: −$10.14.** Negative even before counting opportunity cost on the 9 rejected signals.

**Comparison to baseline:**
- Pre-clamp (session 1): 5/9 = 56% rejects.
- Post-clamp, `SLIPPAGE_TICKS=3` (session 2 post-restart): 3/5 = 60% rejects.
- Post-clamp, `SLIPPAGE_TICKS=6` (this session): 9/17 = 53% rejects.

Statistically indistinguishable — neither the depth clamp (commits 7106b44 + ef527df) nor the wider slippage band moved the needle. Both shipped correctly; both targeted causes that do not actually dominate this market.

**Depth analysis across all 9 post-restart rejects:** every single one had a snapshotted book at decision time with **6× to 457× the required size** at ≤ FOK limit price. The slippage band is not the binding constraint. The race is not "limit doesn't reach high enough." Something else kills the FOK between snapshot and matcher arrival — most likely either (a) the book moves >6 ticks in the 200–500ms signing window (normal for fast 5m BTC moves but the magnitude required is inconsistent with my reading of typical inside-quote volatility), or (b) Polymarket's matcher is consuming the displayed asks differently than we're modeling (binary-market complementary-order pairing where the visible asks aren't the actual taker counterparties), or (c) the WS book feed is feeding stale snapshots that pass our 3s staleness gate but are stale at the matcher.

**Recommendation: Escalate to issue #9 — implement GTC + immediate cancel of unmatched remainder.**

A static slippage band cannot fix this — three independent attempts (clamp, +3 ticks→6 ticks) all failed. With GTC semantics, whatever crosses immediately at our limit fills, and the rest is cancelled — partial fills become real positions instead of the order being killed entirely. The min-fill-ratio gate already shipped in the depth clamp can be reused to skip dust positions. This eliminates the "couldn't be fully filled" failure mode by definition.

Suggested next steps in order:
1. Stop the bot. Continuing to bleed $1+/trade on 53% reject rate is wasteful.
2. Build issue #9 (GTC + cancel) per the design doc's "Alternatives → B" section. Half-day of work; no other dependencies.
3. Re-run a 20-trade session and re-evaluate.
4. Revert `.env` `FOK_SLIPPAGE_TICKS=6` back to default (3) once issue #9 ships, since the wider band is dead weight without a proven benefit.
